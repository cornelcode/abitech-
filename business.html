Commerce & Commercial Strategy — Abitech

The Commercial division transforms Abitech’s products into measurable revenue and lasting partnerships. We combine go-to-market strategy, partnerships, sales operations, and customer success to scale impact across markets.

Overview

The Commercial function at Abitech is responsible for converting innovation into value. Led by the Chief Commercial Officer, the team aligns pricing, sales, partnerships, channel strategy and customer success to grow revenue in both developed and emerging markets.

Commerce works closely with Product, Technology and Marketing to ensure solutions are priced, positioned and delivered in ways that meet customer needs while maintaining sustainable margins.

Sales Strategy & Go-to-Market

Abitech’s sales strategy blends direct enterprise engagement and channel partnerships. The GTM framework defines customer segments, value propositions, sales motions (direct, channel, partner-led), and pilot-to-scale journeys for enterprise and agricultural customers.

The Commercial team develops playbooks for onboarding large accounts, coordinates proof-of-concept phases, and scales successful pilots into long-term contracts with service-level agreements.

Partnerships & Channel Development

Strategic partnerships accelerate distribution and credibility. Abitech builds alliances with retailers, logistics providers, financial institutions, agribusiness cooperatives, and technology vendors to expand reach and deliver integrated solutions.

The Partnerships team structures revenue-sharing models, co-marketing initiatives, and technical integrations to ensure partners can deliver strong customer outcomes and sustainable returns.

Customer Success & Retention

Retention and lifetime value matter. The Customer Success function ensures customers realize value from Abitech solutions, providing onboarding, training, health checks, and a feedback loop to Product and Tech teams for continuous improvement.

Success managers proactively identify expansion opportunities and risks, turning satisfied customers into references and long-term partners.

Market Expansion & New Verticals

Commerce leads market research, country entry strategies, and pilot programs to test demand in new geographies and industry verticals. This includes regulatory analysis, distribution mapping, pricing localization, and go-to-market resource planning.

Expansion work is cross-functional, requiring close coordination with legal, finance, and operations to manage compliance and supply chain readiness.

Pricing, Revenue Models & Commercial Ops

The Commercial team defines pricing strategies—subscription, usage-based, transaction fees, and hybrid models—tailored to customer segments. Commercial Ops handles quoting, contract templates, renewal workflows, payment terms and collections in collaboration with Finance.

Operations support includes CRM hygiene, sales enablement tools, pipeline governance, and playbook enforcement to ensure predictable revenue growth.

Analytics, CRM & Sales Intelligence

Data powers smarter commercial decisions. The Commerce analytics team builds dashboards for pipeline health, conversion rates, customer acquisition cost, and lifetime value. Integrations with CRM and marketing systems provide a single source of truth for opportunity scoring and prioritization.

Insights are used to optimize channel mix, refine sales motions, and inform product-market fit decisions.

Operations Coordination & Fulfillment

Commercial operations ensures orders are fulfilled efficiently—managing logistics, inventory coordination, delivery SLAs, and returns processes. For agritech solutions, this includes cold-chain logistics, farm-to-retailer scheduling, and last-mile distribution planning.

Coordination with Supply Chain and Technology teams ensures customer promises are backed by operational capability.

Commercial Compliance & Risk Management

Commercial deals carry legal, regulatory and financial risk. The CCO ensures contract review, compliance with trade regulations, anti-corruption practices, and alignment with corporate policies. High-value deals undergo risk assessment with Legal and Finance.

This function protects Abitech’s reputation while enabling strategic growth.

Titus Kibiwott

Chief Commercial Officer (CCO)

Titus leads Abitech’s commercial strategy, overseeing sales, partnerships, customer success and revenue operations. He focuses on scaling sustainable revenue streams, building partner ecosystems, and creating world-class customer experiences.

His approach balances aggressive market expansion with disciplined operations to ensure high-quality delivery and profitable growth.

Detailed Commercial Functions & Activities

  • Sales Enablement: Build playbooks, training, CRM templates and collateral for consistent sales execution.
  • Partner Management: Recruit, onboard and manage reseller, distribution and strategic technology partners.
  • Commercial Ops: Maintain quote-to-cash workflows, contract lifecycle and renewal pipelines.
  • Customer Success: Build post-sale programs that increase retention, upsell and customer advocacy.
  • Market Research: Use local insights and competitive analysis to prioritize new country launches and product adaptations.
  • Pricing & Packaging: Define the mix of subscription, transactional and hybrid revenue models across segments.
  • Compliance & Negotiation: Conduct deal-level risk assessments, negotiate terms and secure approvals with Legal/Finance.
  • Revenue Forecasting: Produce monthly and quarterly forecasts tied to pipeline health and lead indicators.
