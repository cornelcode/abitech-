Security Department

The Security Department at Abitech ensures that all company assets — physical, digital, and human — are protected against threats. In an era of growing cybersecurity risks and global uncertainty, the department operates at the core of Abitech’s commitment to safety and resilience.

This department covers multiple layers of protection — from data encryption to employee safety and business continuity planning. Under the leadership of the Chief Security Officer (CSO), Abitech maintains strong systems that safeguard customers, partners, and internal operations.

Linda Martinez

Chief Security Officer (CSO)

Linda oversees the entire security framework of Abitech — from cyber defense to physical protection. She works closely with the CTO and CFO to ensure every layer of business operation runs under secure protocols. Her mission is to create a safe environment for innovation and growth.

With 12+ years in corporate and IT security, Linda leads Abitech’s Security Intelligence Division, guiding the design of predictive systems that prevent threats before they arise.

Core Security Functions

The department’s activities are organized into key focus areas, ensuring complete coverage of both digital and physical threats.

1. Cybersecurity & Data Protection

This division focuses on protecting Abitech’s digital assets, software systems, and client data through encryption, monitoring, and AI-based threat detection. The team also enforces network segmentation, firewall management, and penetration testing.

2. Physical Security & Infrastructure Safety

Physical security ensures all Abitech facilities — offices, data centers, and agricultural branches — are protected through surveillance, access control, and risk management protocols. Trained personnel ensure 24-hour protection for employees and assets.

3. Risk Assessment & Compliance

Abitech’s CSO team conducts continuous risk assessments to identify, analyze, and mitigate potential threats. Regular audits ensure compliance with national and international security standards such as ISO 27001 and GDPR.

4. Incident Response & Business Continuity

The incident response unit manages all security alerts, containment, and post-incident reviews. They also create business continuity plans ensuring operations continue smoothly during crises such as system outages or data breaches.

5. Employee Security Training

Security awareness is central to Abitech’s culture. Employees are trained to detect phishing, handle confidential information safely, and report suspicious behavior promptly.

6. Collaboration with Other Departments

The CSO collaborates with all Abitech divisions — working with the CTO on software protection, with HR on employee security protocols, and with the CFO on fraud prevention and compliance.
